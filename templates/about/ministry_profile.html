Ministry of Planning and International Cooperation

Driving sustainable development and international partnerships for a prosperous Puntland

Overview

The Ministry of Planning and International Cooperation (MOPIC) is a key government institution responsible for planning, coordinating, and monitoring development programs and projects in Puntland State of Somalia. Established to guide the region's development agenda, MOPIC plays a pivotal role in shaping policies, mobilizing resources, and fostering international partnerships to achieve sustainable development goals.

Our Mandate

The Ministry is mandated to formulate, coordinate, and monitor the implementation of development policies, strategies, and programs in line with the Puntland State's development priorities and the national development framework. We serve as the principal advisor to the government on matters related to planning, development cooperation, and international partnerships.

Strategic Objectives

  • Formulate and implement comprehensive development plans aligned with national priorities
  • Coordinate development interventions across sectors and stakeholders
  • Mobilize and manage development resources effectively and efficiently
  • Strengthen international cooperation and partnerships for development
  • Enhance monitoring and evaluation systems for better development results
  • Promote evidence-based policy making through research and data analysis

Core Functions

Development Planning

Formulating and coordinating the implementation of short, medium, and long-term development plans that guide the region's socio-economic transformation.

International Cooperation

Coordinating and managing international development cooperation, partnerships, and foreign aid to support Puntland's development priorities.

Resource Mobilization

Identifying and mobilizing both domestic and international resources to fund development programs and projects across the region.

Monitoring & Evaluation

Tracking and assessing the implementation of development programs to ensure effectiveness, efficiency, and impact.

Our History

The Ministry of Planning and International Cooperation was established in 1998, following the creation of the Puntland State of Somalia. Since its inception, the Ministry has played a central role in the region's reconstruction and development efforts, working closely with local and international partners to rebuild institutions, infrastructure, and communities affected by years of conflict.

1998

Establishment

MOPIC was established as part of the newly formed Puntland State government, with a mandate to coordinate development planning and international cooperation.

2005

First Development Plan

Launched Puntland's first comprehensive development plan, setting the foundation for coordinated development efforts across the region.

2013

Institutional Strengthening

Implemented major capacity building initiatives to enhance the Ministry's ability to coordinate development programs and manage international partnerships.

2020

Digital Transformation

Launched digital platforms for development planning and monitoring, improving transparency and efficiency in service delivery.

Our Impact

Over the years, MOPIC has successfully coordinated numerous development initiatives that have contributed to Puntland's socio-economic progress. From infrastructure development to capacity building and policy formulation, our work has touched the lives of millions of Puntland residents, creating opportunities for sustainable growth and improved livelihoods.
